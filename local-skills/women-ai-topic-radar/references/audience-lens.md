# Audience Lens

Use this file to decide whether an AI topic is truly worth translating for women-focused growth creators.

## Core Audience

The default audience is not "women in general." It is creators speaking to women about growth, life choices, identity, work, relationships, family systems, parenting, learning, and emotional wellbeing.

This audience usually responds to topics that answer one of these questions:

- Will this change how I work, learn, or earn?
- Will this reduce or increase my daily pressure?
- Does this affect family routines, parenting, education, or care work?
- Does this change what women are expected to know, do, or compete on?
- Is there a new anxiety, aspiration, or social comparison pressure worth interpreting?

## Strong Fits

Usually keep topics that connect to:

- career growth, side hustles, or creator work
- education, learning tools, or parenting support
- family organization, care work, and household efficiency
- emotional labor, identity pressure, confidence, or self-worth
- beauty, health, body image, or relationship discourse when AI changes the conversation
- new creator behaviors on public accounts, Xiaohongshu, or adjacent lifestyle platforms

## Weak Fits

Usually drop or down-rank topics like:

- raw model leaderboard movement
- enterprise infrastructure updates with no consumer effect
- funding, org charts, or hiring shifts with no audience implication
- purely developer-facing tooling unless it obviously changes creator workflows

## Translation Test

Keep a topic only if you can complete at least one of these sentences clearly:

- "For women-focused growth audiences, this matters because..."
- "A creator could turn this into a useful piece by exploring..."
- "This changes daily life, parenting, work, or emotional expectations in that it..."

If you cannot finish any of them without stretching, drop the topic.

## Family And Parenting Guidance

Family and parenting are important secondary lenses, not mandatory tags.

Use them only when the link is concrete, such as:

- homework or learning tools
- screen-time negotiation
- family scheduling or household labor
- child safety, literacy, or developmental concerns
- intergenerational tech understanding

Do not force every AI topic into a parenting narrative.

## Tone Guidance

Prefer framing that is:

- practical
- emotionally aware
- skeptical of hype
- useful for content planning

Avoid framing that is:

- fear-mongering
- overly techno-utopian
- patronizing toward women or mothers
- dependent on unverified "everyone is doing this now" claims
