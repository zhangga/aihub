# Topic Angles

Use this file to convert raw AI buzz into creator-ready directions for women-focused growth content.

## Angle Bank

Try one or more of these lenses for each surviving topic:

- career impact: how this changes work, income, skills, or employability
- daily efficiency: how this changes schedules, chores, planning, or mental load
- emotional interpretation: what pressure, desire, identity shift, or comparison it creates
- family system: how this affects care work, parenting, learning, or household decisions
- creator economy: how public-account or Xiaohongshu creators may package or monetize it
- values debate: what people are really arguing about beyond the product itself

## Public-Account Direction

Public-account ideas usually work best when the angle is:

- interpretive
- evidence-backed
- slightly slower and deeper
- useful for decision-making or emotional clarity

Good output shapes:

- trend analysis
- myth versus reality
- "what this means for ordinary people"
- "what creators are missing in this conversation"

## Xiaohongshu Direction

Xiaohongshu ideas usually work best when the angle is:

- concrete
- personal
- emotionally legible
- easy to save, share, or imitate

Good output shapes:

- practical checklist
- personal perspective
- before-after lifestyle comparison
- "who should try this / who should avoid this"

## Questions To Ask Per Topic

Ask:

1. What are people actually reacting to: utility, identity, fear, status, or novelty?
2. What is the strongest life-level consequence?
3. Does this topic invite action, debate, reassurance, or caution?
4. Is the creator adding interpretation, practical help, or merely repeating the news?

## Angle Examples

### If the topic is a new AI productivity tool

Possible directions:

- does it genuinely reduce invisible labor for women, or add another expectation?
- can it help creators produce faster without flattening voice?
- is the appeal practical, aspirational, or performative?

### If the topic is AI in education or parenting

Possible directions:

- where does it help versus replace attention?
- what should parents know before copying the trend?
- what are the age, safety, or dependency concerns?

### If the topic is AI-generated beauty, image, or relationship content

Possible directions:

- does it reshape self-image, dating expectations, or social comparison?
- is the audience reacting from curiosity, insecurity, or resistance?
- what should creators avoid amplifying irresponsibly?

## Risk Reminder

When generating final angles:

- do not overclaim transformation
- do not turn weak evidence into certainty
- do not package moral panic as insight
- do not assume all women or all mothers respond the same way
