# Scoring Rules

Use these rules to rank candidate topics and decide what belongs in the final brief.

## Five Core Dimensions

Score each topic from 0 to 3 on:

- `social_heat`
- `audience_fit`
- `content_yield`
- `platform_fit`
- `freshness`

Maximum score: `15`

## Dimension Definitions

### Social Heat

- `3`: clearly hot across multiple platforms or dominant on one major platform with visible spillover
- `2`: strong discussion on one platform or moderate discussion on several platforms
- `1`: niche or expert-only discussion
- `0`: weak signal

### Audience Fit

- `3`: directly tied to women's growth, work, family life, parenting, emotion, identity, or self-improvement
- `2`: relevant after light translation by a creator
- `1`: possible but strained fit
- `0`: no meaningful fit for the target audience

### Content Yield

- `3`: easily supports multiple strong angles, opinions, examples, or comparisons
- `2`: supports one clear and useful angle
- `1`: thin topic with little room beyond repetition
- `0`: not enough material to justify coverage

### Platform Fit

- `3`: naturally works for both public-account depth and Xiaohongshu packaging
- `2`: strong for one platform and acceptable for the other
- `1`: only works with heavy reframing
- `0`: poor fit for both

### Freshness

- `3`: new within the report window and still accelerating
- `2`: recent and still discussed, but already stabilizing
- `1`: fading or mostly recycled
- `0`: stale

## Risk Screen

Evaluate risk separately after scoring.

Common risks:

- exaggerated productivity or income claims
- education or parenting panic hooks without evidence
- ethically sensitive uses involving children, body image, relationships, or mental health
- platform rumor without enough confirmation
- topics already exhausted by the creator niche

Use risk to downgrade, defer, or drop an item.

## Keep Or Drop Guidance

- `12-15`: strong immediate candidate
- `9-11`: include if it improves coverage or gives a distinct angle
- `6-8`: usually move to watchlist
- `0-5`: drop

Drop immediately if:

- `audience_fit < 2`
- the core claim cannot be verified well enough
- the topic only sounds important because of AI industry excitement

## Tie-Breakers

When two topics score similarly, prefer the one that:

- carries clearer real-life implications
- has more specific creator framing
- can be packaged differently on public accounts and Xiaohongshu
- is less dependent on hype language
